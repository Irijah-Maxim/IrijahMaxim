    • A Proud Member of the BukSU-ROTC Unit

    • My time in the ROTC unit has been well spent. In the unit, we are taught how to be disciplined and respectful. We are taught about AFP history, hierarchy, their code, the Military Professonalism Oath, the Pledge of Loyalty, and the Standards of Conduct of a Soldier. We are also taught to find value in ourselves and the dangers we will encounter in the military, as well as basic life support. Here we also do physical activities, which currently mostly consist of drilling and following commands. The ROTC, while a non-mentally taxing NSTP course, puts great emphasis on discipline and compliance. Even if the sun is up high, even if the ground we stand on is wet, even if the air is cold, if we are told to drill, we will comply. It definitely helped me to feel less discontented about “questionable” situations and surroundings; it also helped a lot that the officers were reasonable people and would take into account the limits and complications of some cadets.
